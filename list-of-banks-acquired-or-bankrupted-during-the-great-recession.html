Financial Institutions Affected During the Great Recession (2007-2009)

Crisis Overview: 2007-2009 • Global financial meltdown • $2+ trillion in bank losses • 500+ US bank failures • Major government bailouts • Worldwide economic contraction

The Great Recession witnessed the most severe financial crisis since the Great Depression, resulting in hundreds of bank failures, forced mergers, and government nationalizations worldwide. This comprehensive list documents the most significant institutional casualties across all major economies, analyzing the causes and consequences of each failure.

Icelandic Banking Collapse

Iceland's entire banking system collapsed in October 2008:

  • Glitnir: Nationalized 09/29/2008
  • Landsbanki: Collapsed 10/07/2008
  • Kaupthing: Failed 10/09/2008

Total impact: $85 billion in defaulted debt (7× Iceland's GDP)

Root Causes of Bank Failures

Subprime Mortgage Crisis

  • Widespread defaults on high-risk loans
  • Collapse of mortgage-backed securities
  • 30% decline in US housing prices
  • $1.3 trillion in lost home equity

Overleveraging

  • Average 30:1 leverage at investment banks
  • Off-balance sheet vehicles (SIVs)
  • Credit default swaps exposure
  • Inadequate capital reserves

Liquidity Crisis

  • Interbank lending freeze
  • Commercial paper market collapse
  • Fire sale asset valuations
  • Credit rating downgrades

Long-Term Consequences

  • Dodd-Frank Act (2010): Major US financial reform
  • Basel III: Stricter international capital requirements
  • Volcker Rule: Restrictions on proprietary trading
  • ECB Banking Supervision: Eurozone oversight created
  • Consumer Protection: New agencies established worldwide
