People's Trust Insurance Company: Florida's Home Insurance Specialist

Key Company Facts: Founded 2008 • Headquarters: Deerfield Beach, FL • 110,000+ policyholders • 350+ employees • Financial Stability Rating: "A" (Exceptional) • Specializes in Florida homeowners insurance

People's Trust Insurance Company has established itself as a leading provider of homeowners insurance in Florida since its founding in 2008. As a privately held company specializing in property insurance, People's Trust has grown to become the eleventh largest homeowners insurer in Florida, serving over 110,000 policyholders across the state with innovative coverage solutions and rapid claims response.

Company History and Milestones

2008: Founding Years

People's Trust Insurance was co-founded by insurance industry veterans George Schaeffer and Michael Gold with the vision of creating a more responsive homeowners insurance provider specifically designed for Florida's unique risks. The company launched during a challenging period in Florida's insurance market following several catastrophic hurricane seasons.

2012-2014: Rapid Growth Phase

The South Florida Business Journal recognized People's Trust as the second-fastest growing company in South Florida during this period. The company expanded its workforce to over 350 employees, with 300 based at its Deerfield Beach headquarters.

2013: Governor's Innovators in Business Award

People's Trust received this prestigious Florida award recognizing companies that significantly contribute to the state's economic growth and diversification through innovation in their industry.

2014: Leadership Transition

Following the passing of co-founder Michael Gold, the company underwent a leadership transition while maintaining its growth trajectory and commitment to policyholder service.

2015-Present: Market Consolidation

The company solidified its position as a top 15 Florida homeowners insurer, continuing to innovate in claims processing and policyholder services while maintaining financial stability.

Legal and Regulatory Matters

Notable Lawsuits

In November 2013, a Florida appellate court upheld a $766,258.06 jury award to policyholder Raymond Roddy for a house fire claim. The case established important precedents regarding:

  • Insurer obligations during claims investigations
  • Timeliness requirements for claims processing
  • Policyholder rights in disputed claims
