Complete Guide to CNO Financial Group

CNO Financial Group, Inc. (NYSE: CNO) stands as a significant player in the U.S. insurance market, specializing in serving middle-income Americans through its portfolio of life insurance, annuities, and supplemental health products. Headquartered in Carmel, Indiana, the company has evolved through several transformations to become a focused provider of insurance solutions for the "mass middle market" and senior citizens.

Corporate Overview

Key facts about CNO Financial Group:

Essential Company Data:
  • Founded: 1979 (as Security National of Indiana)
  • Headquarters: Carmel, Indiana
  • Employees: Approximately 3,300
  • NYSE Symbol: CNO
  • Market Cap: $3.2 billion (as of 2023)
  • Customers Served: Over 4 million policyholders

Historical Evolution

CNO's journey through key transformations:

Early Years (1979-1990s)

  • Founded by Stephen Hilbert as Security National of Indiana
  • Acquired Consolidated National Life Insurance (1982)
  • Renamed Conseco (from CONsolidated and SEcurity COrporation)
  • Went public in 1985 (NASDAQ initially, later NYSE)

Expansion Era (1990s-2000)

  • Aggressive acquisition strategy including Colonial Penn and Bankers Life
  • Purchased Green Tree Financial (1998) - later proved problematic
  • Reached Fortune 500 status at peak
  • Faced financial difficulties leading to Chapter 11 bankruptcy (2002)

Restructuring and Rebirth (2003-Present)

  • Emergence from bankruptcy as Conseco (2003)
  • Divested non-core assets including Green Tree
  • Rebranded as CNO Financial Group (2010)
  • Focused strategy on core insurance businesses

Business Segments

CNO operates through three primary business segments:

Bankers Life

The company's largest subsidiary focuses on senior market:

  • Founded in 1879 in Chicago
  • Acquired by Conseco in 1982
  • Specializes in Medicare supplements and senior life insurance
  • Distributed through 4,000+ career agents
  • Generates approximately 50% of CNO's revenue

Washington National

Middle-market supplemental health and life products:

  • Founded in 1885
  • Focus on accident, critical illness, and hospital indemnity
  • Distributed through independent agents
  • Known for "Premier Choice" fixed whole life product

Colonial Penn

Direct-to-consumer insurance provider:

  • Acquired in 1997
  • Specializes in simplified-issue life insurance
  • Heavily marketed through TV (Alex Trebek era ads)
  • "Guaranteed Acceptance" whole life product
  • Targets customers aged 50-85

Product Portfolio

CNO's insurance offerings by category:

Life Insurance Products

  • Term Life: Temporary coverage (Bankers Life)
  • Whole Life: Permanent coverage with cash value
  • Final Expense: Simplified-issue (Colonial Penn)
  • Universal Life: Flexible premium products

Annuities

  • Fixed Annuities: Guaranteed interest rates
  • Indexed Annuities: Market-linked growth
  • Immediate Annuities: Income stream products

Supplemental Health

  • Medicare Supplements: Gap coverage for seniors
  • Critical Illness: Lump-sum benefit policies
  • Accident Insurance: Injury-related benefits
  • Hospital Indemnity: Fixed daily benefits

Distribution Channels

How CNO products reach customers:

Career Agency System

  • Bankers Life's network of 4,000+ exclusive agents
  • Local offices in major markets nationwide
  • Focus on face-to-face senior market sales

Independent Agents

  • Washington National products sold through brokers
  • Works with 30,000+ independent agents
  • Online portal for agent support

Direct Response

  • Colonial Penn's TV and direct mail marketing
  • 1-800 number and online enrollment
  • No medical exam products for seniors
