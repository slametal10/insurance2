American Council of Life Insurers (ACLI)

Overview

The American Council of Life Insurers (ACLI) is the premier trade association representing the life insurance industry in the United States. Headquartered in Washington, D.C., ACLI serves as the unified voice for 275 member companies that provide financial protection and security to approximately 90 million American families. The organization plays a critical role in shaping public policy, advocating for favorable legislation, and promoting the value of life insurance products.

History and Background

Founded in 1976 through the merger of two predecessor organizations, ACLI has grown to become one of the most influential insurance trade associations in the nation. The organization traces its roots back to the early 20th century when the need for a unified voice for life insurers became apparent as government regulation of the industry increased.

ACLI's headquarters at 101 Constitution Avenue NW in Washington, D.C. positions it strategically near the nation's legislative and regulatory centers, allowing for effective advocacy and policy influence.

Member Companies and Industry Impact

ACLI member companies represent approximately 90% of the life insurance industry's assets in the United States. These companies offer a comprehensive range of financial protection products including:

  • Term and permanent life insurance policies
  • Annuities and retirement income products
  • Disability income insurance
  • Long-term care insurance
  • Supplemental health benefits
  • Paid family leave solutions

Collectively, ACLI members hold more than $7 trillion in assets, making the life insurance industry one of the largest sources of investment capital in the nation. These investments support infrastructure projects, create jobs, and contribute to economic growth.

Leadership

Susan K. Neely has served as ACLI's president and CEO since 2018. With extensive experience in both government and association leadership, Neely previously served as president and CEO of the American Beverage Association and as Assistant Secretary for Public Affairs at the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.

Prior leadership includes:

  • Dirk Kempthorne (2010-2018): Former Governor of Idaho and U.S. Secretary of the Interior
  • Frank Keating (2003-2011): Former Governor of Oklahoma

Key Initiatives and Advocacy

ACLI focuses on several core areas of advocacy and public policy:

1. Retirement Security

ACLI advocates for policies that strengthen retirement security, including promoting annuities as part of retirement plans and supporting legislation that makes it easier for Americans to save for retirement.

2. Tax Policy

The organization works to preserve the tax treatment of life insurance products and protect the death benefit from taxation. ACLI also advocates for policies that encourage retirement savings.

3. Regulation and Solvency

ACLI engages with state and federal regulators to promote sound regulatory policies that ensure insurer solvency while allowing companies to innovate and serve customers effectively.

4. Healthcare Policy

The organization works on issues related to long-term care, disability income, and supplemental health insurance products.

Legislative Achievements

ACLI has been instrumental in several significant legislative victories for the life insurance industry:

Insurance Capital Standards Clarification Act of 2014

ACLI actively lobbied for this bill (S. 2270; 113th Congress), which clarified that insurance products should not be subject to banking capital standards. The organization ran advertisements in major Washington, D.C. newspapers to build support for this legislation.

SECURE Act

ACLI played a key role in the passage of the Setting Every Community Up for Retirement Enhancement (SECURE) Act in 2019, which included provisions making it easier for employers to offer annuities in 401(k) plans.

Tax Cuts and Jobs Act

ACLI successfully advocated for maintaining the tax-deferred status of life insurance products during the 2017 tax reform process.

Research and Publications

ACLI produces several important industry resources:

Life Insurers Fact Book

This annual publication provides comprehensive statistics and analysis of industry trends, including:

  • Market penetration and coverage statistics
  • Product trends and innovations
  • Demographic analysis of policyholders
  • Economic impact of the industry

Policy White Papers

ACLI regularly publishes in-depth research papers on critical issues affecting the industry, such as retirement security, regulatory reform, and healthcare policy.

Consumer Education

ACLI operates several consumer education initiatives designed to help Americans understand and access life insurance products:

  • "Life Happens" awareness campaign
  • Retirement planning resources
  • Disability income insurance education
  • Long-term care planning tools

Industry Events and Conferences

ACLI hosts several major industry events each year:

  • Annual Conference: Brings together industry leaders, regulators, and policymakers
  • Legal & Regulatory Conference: Focuses on compliance and regulatory issues
  • State Legislative Conference: Addresses state-level policy matters

International Engagement

While primarily focused on U.S. policy, ACLI also engages internationally through:

  • Participation in the International Insurance Society
  • Collaboration with global insurance associations
  • Monitoring of international regulatory developments

Recent Policy Priorities

Current ACLI advocacy efforts focus on:

  • Expanding access to retirement income products
  • Modernizing state insurance regulations
  • Promoting diversity and inclusion in the industry
  • Addressing climate-related risks and opportunities
  • Supporting pandemic-related insurance solutions

Economic Impact

The life insurance industry represented by ACLI has significant economic impact:

  • Provides direct employment to over 2.5 million Americans
  • Manages $7 trillion in assets supporting the economy
  • Pays out billions in benefits annually to policyholders
  • Invests heavily in infrastructure and community development

Future Challenges and Opportunities

The life insurance industry faces several emerging challenges and opportunities:

  • Demographic shifts and aging population
  • Technological innovation and insurtech
  • Changing workforce dynamics
  • Climate change and catastrophic risks
  • Cybersecurity threats
  • Changing consumer expectations

ACLI continues to work with policymakers, regulators, and member companies to address these challenges while ensuring Americans have access to essential financial protection products.
