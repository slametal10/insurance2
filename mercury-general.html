Mercury General Corporation: America's Auto Insurance Specialist

Company Snapshot: Founded 1961 • HQ: Los Angeles, CA • $4B+ assets • 4,500 employees • 8,000+ agents • 11 operating states • NYSE: MCY • Specializes in auto and homeowners insurance

Mercury General Corporation has established itself as a leading provider of personal and commercial insurance products across the United States. With its founding principle of offering affordable insurance alternatives, Mercury has grown from a California-focused auto insurer to a multi-line insurance organization serving customers through independent agents in 11 states.

Company History and Milestones

1961: Founding Vision

George Joseph, a Harvard graduate and World War II veteran, founded Mercury Insurance with $50,000 in capital. His experience as a navigator on 50 combat missions shaped his disciplined approach to risk assessment and pricing.

1964-1972: California Expansion

Mercury established its first Orange County office in 1964, followed by expansion into San Fernando Valley (1966), San Diego (1966), and Northern California (1968). By 1970, the company was writing $1 million in premiums monthly.

1972-1985: Product Diversification

The company launched its homeowners insurance line in 1972 and created Mercury Insurance Company in 1977 to expand its auto insurance offerings. This period saw Mercury develop its distinctive agent-focused distribution model.

1985-1996: Geographic Expansion

Mercury expanded beyond California for the first time, entering Georgia (1985), Texas (1989), and Arizona (1991). The company went public in 1996, trading on the NYSE under symbol MCY.

2000-Present: Modern Era

Mercury continued expanding to Florida (2004), Virginia (2007), New Jersey (2010), and most recently Illinois (2018). The company has maintained its focus on auto insurance while growing its homeowners and commercial lines.
