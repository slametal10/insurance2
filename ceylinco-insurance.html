Ceylinco Insurance: Sri Lanka's Insurance Market Leader

Founded: 1939 (as Ceylon Insurance Company)

Headquarters: Colombo, Sri Lanka

Parent Company: Ceylinco Holdings PLC

Listed: Colombo Stock Exchange (1988)

Revenue: LKR 45.2 billion (2022)

Market Share: ~25% of Sri Lankan insurance market

Employees: Over 5,000

Branches: 200+ across Sri Lanka

Company Overview

Ceylinco Insurance, operating under Ceylinco Holdings PLC, stands as the largest private sector insurance provider in Sri Lanka. The company has established itself as a diversified financial services group with significant operations in:

  • Life and general insurance
  • Healthcare services
  • Renewable energy projects
  • Educational institutions
  • Real estate development

The company serves over 2 million policyholders across Sri Lanka and has expanded its operations internationally through strategic partnerships.

Historical Timeline

1939: Founding Years

Ceylon Insurance Company was established by Hugh C. Weeresekere during British colonial rule, becoming one of the first indigenous insurance providers in Ceylon.

1964: Transition to Kotelawala Leadership

Justin Kotelawala took over management of the company, guiding it through the nationalization era of Sri Lanka's insurance industry.

1987: Rebirth as Ceylinco

Following insurance sector liberalization, the company was rebranded as Ceylinco Limited and began its modern expansion.

1988: Public Listing

The company was listed on the Colombo Stock Exchange, marking its transition to a publicly traded entity.

1990s: Rapid Expansion

Ceylinco grew to become market leader through aggressive branch network expansion and product innovation.

2000s: Diversification

The company expanded into healthcare, education and renewable energy sectors while strengthening insurance operations.

Present Day

Ceylinco maintains market leadership despite economic challenges in Sri Lanka, continuing to innovate in insurance products and services.

Financial Performance

Ceylinco has demonstrated consistent financial strength despite Sri Lanka's challenging economic environment:

  • Gross Written Premium (2022): LKR 45.2 billion (~$140 million)
  • Investment Portfolio: LKR 120 billion in assets under management
  • Claims Settlement Ratio: 98.7% for life insurance, 95.2% for general insurance
  • Solvency Ratio: Maintains well above regulatory requirements

Market Position and Competitors

Ceylinco maintains approximately 25% market share in Sri Lanka's insurance sector. Major competitors include:

  • State-owned: Sri Lanka Insurance Corporation
  • Private competitors: Janashakthi Insurance, Union Assurance, AIA Sri Lanka
  • International players: Allianz, AIA, Prudential

Corporate Structure and Subsidiaries

Ceylinco Holdings PLC operates through several key subsidiaries:

  • Ceylinco Life Insurance: Dedicated life insurance operations
  • Ceylinco General Insurance: Non-life insurance products
  • Ceylinco Healthcare: Hospital and medical services
  • Ceylinco Education: Private educational institutions
  • Ceylinco Renewable Energy: Solar and hydro power projects
