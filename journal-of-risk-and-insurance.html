Journal of Risk and Insurance: Premier Publication in Insurance Scholarship

Journal Essentials: Quarterly publication • Established 1933 • Impact Factor: 1.803 (2020) • Publisher: Wiley • Editor-in-Chief: Joan T. Schmit • ISSN: 1539-6975 (online) • 0022-4367 (print)

The Journal of Risk and Insurance (JRI) stands as one of the most respected academic publications in the field of insurance economics and risk management. Published quarterly by Wiley on behalf of the American Risk and Insurance Association (ARIA), the journal serves as a vital platform for scholarly discourse, presenting cutting-edge research that shapes industry practices and regulatory policies worldwide.

Historical Development and Evolution

1933: Founding Years

The journal originated as the Proceedings of the Annual Meeting of the American Association of University Teachers of Insurance, reflecting the academic community's need for a formal platform to share insurance research and pedagogy.

1937: First Renaming

Rebranded as the Journal of The American Association of University Teachers of Insurance, the publication began establishing its identity as a scholarly journal rather than just conference proceedings.

1954-1956: The Review of Insurance Studies

During this brief period, the journal adopted a name that emphasized its research focus, signaling its maturation into a true academic publication.

1957-1963: The Journal of Insurance

The simplified title reflected the growing scope of insurance as an academic discipline and the journal's expanding coverage of industry developments.

1964-Present: Journal of Risk and Insurance

The current title, adopted in 1964, incorporates "Risk" to acknowledge the growing importance of risk management as a distinct field of study intertwined with insurance.
