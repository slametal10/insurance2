Bankers Life: Specialized Insurance for Retirement Planning

Bankers Life and Casualty Company, operating as Bankers Life, is a premier provider of insurance and financial services specifically designed for middle-income Americans approaching or in retirement. Headquartered in Chicago, Illinois, the company has built an 90-year reputation for serving the unique needs of seniors through comprehensive insurance products and personalized service.

Company Overview

As a subsidiary of CNO Financial Group (NYSE: CNO), Bankers Life focuses exclusively on the retirement market with:

  • 1.4 million active policyholders across the U.S. and Canada
  • Over 5,000 licensed insurance agents nationwide
  • 320+ local offices providing face-to-face service
  • $3.2 billion in annual premium income (2022 figures)
  • A.M. Best financial strength rating of B++ (Good)

Corporate History and Milestones

1932: Founding Years

Bankers Life and Casualty Company was established in Chicago as a mutual life insurance company during the Great Depression. The company differentiated itself by keeping overhead costs extremely low to offer more affordable premiums than competitors.

1935: MacArthur Acquisition

After financial difficulties, the company was purchased for $2,500 by John D. MacArthur, who merged it with his existing Marquette Life Insurance Company. This marked the beginning of the company's turnaround under MacArthur's unconventional leadership style.

1950s-1960s: Expansion Era

Bankers Life grew significantly by targeting middle-income customers overlooked by larger insurers. The company pioneered direct-response marketing and built a national sales force during this period.

1978: MacArthur Foundation

Following John D. MacArthur's death, his ownership stake in Bankers Life was transferred to the newly created John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation, which became one of America's largest philanthropic organizations.

1992: CNO Financial Group Formation

The company became part of the newly formed Conseco (later renamed CNO Financial Group), establishing its current corporate structure while maintaining the Bankers Life brand.

2000s: Senior Market Specialization

Bankers Life refined its focus exclusively on retirement-aged consumers, developing specialized Medicare and long-term care products while expanding its local agent network.

Present Day

Bankers Life continues as a market leader in senior insurance solutions, consistently ranked among the top providers of Medicare Supplement insurance and maintaining strong financial ratings.
