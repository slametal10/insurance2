Axis Bank

Axis Bank Limited, formerly known as UTI Bank (1993–2007), is an Indian multinational banking and financial services company headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. As of 2023, it ranks as India's third largest private sector bank by assets and fourth largest by market capitalization. The bank provides comprehensive financial services to large and mid-size corporations, small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and retail customers through its extensive network of branches and digital platforms.

Corporate Overview

Axis Bank operates as a full-service commercial bank offering a wide spectrum of financial products and services. The bank's current market position reflects its successful transition from a government-promoted entity to a professionally managed private sector bank with strong institutional and retail shareholder participation.

As of the latest available data, the shareholding pattern of Axis Bank is as follows:

  • Promoters and Promoter Group: 30.81% (including United India Insurance Company Limited, Oriental Insurance Company Limited, and other government insurance entities)
  • Public Shareholding: 69.19% (comprising mutual funds, foreign institutional investors, banks, insurance companies, corporate bodies, and individual investors)

Historical Development

1993-1994: Foundation

The bank was incorporated on 3 December 1993 as UTI Bank, established as part of Unit Trust of India (UTI), a Government of India entity. It opened its registered office in Ahmedabad and corporate office in Mumbai. The inaugural branch was launched on 2 April 1994 in Ahmedabad by then Finance Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh.

2001: Proposed Merger

UTI Bank agreed to merge with Global Trust Bank, but the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) withheld approval. This proved fortuitous when in 2004, RBI placed Global Trust under moratorium leading to its eventual merger with Oriental Bank of Commerce.

2007: Rebranding

The bank underwent a major rebranding exercise, changing its name from UTI Bank to Axis Bank on 30 July 2007. This marked its transformation into a completely private sector bank.

2013: International Expansion

Axis Bank established its first overseas branch in Singapore, followed by branches in Dubai, Hong Kong, and Shanghai, marking its emergence as a multinational banking institution.

2019: Digital Transformation

The bank launched Axis ASAP 2.0, a digital banking platform featuring voice banking and AI-powered services, significantly enhancing its digital capabilities.

2022: Subsidiary in GIFT City

Axis Bank established its international banking subsidiary in Gujarat International Finance Tec-City (GIFT City), expanding its offshore banking services.
