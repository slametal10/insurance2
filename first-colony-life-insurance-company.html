First Colony Life Insurance Company: A Legacy of Innovation

Key Facts: Founded in 1955 in Lynchburg, VA, First Colony Life pioneered independent broker distribution before being acquired by GE Capital in 1996 and ultimately becoming part of Genworth Financial in 2007.

Founding and Early Growth (1955-1965)

First Colony Life Insurance Company was established in 1955 by Edwin Horner in Lynchburg, Virginia, with a focus on term life insurance products. The company initially specialized in impaired risk underwriting, serving clients who had difficulty obtaining coverage through traditional insurers.

Horner's vision for First Colony Life included several innovative approaches:

  • Independent distribution: Leveraging brokerage general agencies (BGAs) rather than captive agents
  • Simplified underwriting: Streamlined processes for term life products
  • Regional focus: Serving the southeastern U.S. market initially
"Ed Horner saw the potential of independent distribution when most insurers still relied entirely on their own sales forces. This vision would transform the life insurance industry." - Insurance History Journal, 2005

Early Milestones

1955 - Founded by Edwin Horner in Lynchburg
1957 - Develops innovative term life product for middle-class families
1960 - Expands to 10-state southeastern footprint

Expansion and Ownership Changes (1965-1996)

The company underwent significant transformation under the leadership of George Stewart, who became president in 1965:

Key Developments

Year Event Impact
1963 Acquired by Broadway-Hale Stores Provided capital for expansion
1965 Los Angeles Investment Co. purchase George Stewart becomes president
1968 Moves to renovated Guggenheimer building Establishes permanent Lynchburg HQ
1970s National expansion Becomes top 50 life insurer

During this period, First Colony Life:

  • Expanded its product line to include whole life and annuities
  • Grew its BGA network to over 50,000 agents
  • Developed innovative marketing materials for independent agents

The Guggenheimer Building

First Colony's renovated headquarters at 1000 Main Street featured:

  • 50,000 square feet of office space
  • Modern facilities for policy processing
  • Training center for agents
  • Remained company HQ until GE acquisition

The GE Capital Era (1996-2004)

GE Financial Assurance's 1996 acquisition marked a new chapter:

Growth Under GE

  • Workforce doubled from 800 to 1,500 employees
  • New customer service center opened in Lynchburg
  • Annuity products became major focus
  • Technology investments modernized operations

Distribution Network

First Colony maintained its BGA focus with:

  • 135,000 independent agents nationwide
  • Specialized marketing support
  • Competitive commission structures
  • Strong underwriting turnaround times

Transition to Genworth (2004-2007)

GE's spin-off of Genworth Financial included First Colony Life:

Integration Process

  1. 2004: Genworth Financial created as GE spin-off
  2. 2005-2006: Operational integration begins
  3. 2007: Legal merger completed (January 1)

Legacy: Though the First Colony name was retired in 2007, many of its products, distribution methods, and underwriting approaches continue within Genworth's life and annuity operations.
